# Decision 004 — Naming: From DK PM OS → HYVE → Arthur

**Date:** 2026-03-22  
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Agents Involved:** Claude (CTO Advisor)  
**Director:** DK (Founder & Product Lead)  
**Outcome:** SHIPPED — Full rebrand deployed in v6

---

### Context

DK recognized that the product name "HYVE · Mission Control" created brand confusion — he also had "HYVE Myanmar," a separate venture. Hiring managers and investors reviewing his portfolio would confuse the two. DK initiated the rename and came to the conversation with a specific direction.

---

### DK's Vision

DK proposed "Arthur" and immediately explained the layered meaning: King Arthur and the Knights of the Round Table. But the deeper reason — Arthur is DK's son's name.

DK's brand instinct was precise: the Round Table metaphor maps directly to Arthur MCS's decision architecture. The king doesn't fight every battle — he assembles the best knights, gives them a shared mission, and makes decisions at the table. That's exactly what a PM does. The AI agents (Claude, Carter, Gemini, Daniel) are the knights — each with different strengths, reporting to one table. DK is Arthur.

When Claude presented alternatives (Arthur HQ, Camelot, Roundtable), DK chose decisively: "Arthur. One word." Then refined the full product name himself: "Arthur · Mission Control System" — adding "System" to reinforce that this is a complete operating system, not just a dashboard.

---

### The Decision

**What DK chose:**  
Company name: Arthur. Product: Arthur · Mission Control System. Logo: interlocking hexagon in navy (#1B4F72) + orange (#E67E22). Tagline: "A Hypothesis-Driven Operating System for Product Leaders."

**DK's narrative for investors:**  
"I named it after my son and the king who built a system where the best decisions came from bringing the right people to the table."

---

### Lessons / Patterns

**DK builds brand with personal meaning + strategic metaphor.** The name works on three levels: personal (son), metaphorical (Round Table = decision gates), and practical (one word, memorable, easy to search). This layered brand thinking is what separates a product name from a company identity.
