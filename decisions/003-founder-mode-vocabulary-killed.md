# Decision 003 — "Founder Mode" Vocabulary Permanently Killed

**Date:** 2026-03-25  
**Status:** DECIDED  
**Agents Involved:** Claude (CTO Advisor)  
**Director:** DK (Founder & Product Lead)  
**Outcome:** KILLED — PM III+ vocabulary retained across all stages

---

### Context

After analyzing ChatPRD's market and Reddit user feedback, Claude proposed a UX copy pass: add toggleable "Founder-Friendly" labels to Stage 0 fields. "Problem Hypothesis" would become "What problem are you solving?" The rationale was accessibility for non-PM founders.

---

### DK's Immediate Decision

DK shut this down decisively: "No. We are not going there. We are keeping traditional PM terms. Founder usage will be less than 5% of total users."

DK's reasoning came from direct experience as a two-time founder: "I was a founder of 2 startups. I know how it is. But I need to know actual terms. Otherwise, as the team grows and you start hiring real PMs — senior ones from big tech — you lost them in translation."

DK then broadened the directive beyond vocabulary into a product philosophy: "I don't want any methodology and framework changes. Stick to PM Playbook, period. What we want to do is close the gap on functionality — integrations, data flows, connections."

---

### The Decision

**What DK decided:**  
PM III+ vocabulary stays permanent. No Founder Mode. The Playbook terminology IS the product terminology. All development energy goes to functionality gaps, not methodology modifications.

**Key tradeoff DK accepted:**  
Some founders without PM experience may find the interface initially intimidating. DK's bet: methodology-as-mentorship (learning proper PM terms through using Arthur) serves founders better long-term than watered-down labels.

**What this established:**  
"The Playbook is locked" became the permanent product principle. No agent can propose methodology or framework changes — only functionality improvements.

---

### Lessons / Patterns

**DK makes decisive calls from lived experience.** This wasn't analysis paralysis or seeking consensus — DK recognized immediately that founder-friendly labels would dilute Arthur's credibility with the ICP. Two-time founder experience gave him the confidence to reject a well-reasoned proposal in seconds.

**Product leaders define what's off the table.** By declaring the Playbook locked, DK eliminated an entire category of future scope creep. Every agent now knows: no methodology changes, only functionality gaps.
